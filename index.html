Be Kind, Be Random, Be Awesome

A Student Run Makerthon

Web Development

We will be hosting workshops on HTML, CSS, and Javasrcipt as well as web development using Ruby on Rails. Having learnt some basics, you can expect to make small interactive web apps!

Game Design

Scratch and Hopscotch are interactive tools to teach yourself how to code. They are easy to learn and allow you to make games in the matter of hours. We will have several speakers giving workshops on conceptualizing and making your game.

Graphics

We will be hosting workshops in using processing. Processing is a powerful coding library that enables its users to create elaborate computer art. If interested we will also be providing assistance in the projects.

Robotics

We will be holding workshops on using the Arduino toolkit to make fun and complex robotics and physical computing projects. We will be providing arduino kits and extentions. Further, we will have mentors to guide you.

Mobile Development

We will be holding workshops to get you up to speed with iOS and android development. Our workshops will include making a sample project and there will be mentors to help you cross any roadblocks.

About Us

Random Makerthon of Kindness

Tech@NYU is a community passionate builders, programmers and entrepreneurs that hope to make the world a better place by one step at a time.

In order to accomplish this, we are hosting a makerthon for kids (9 to 15 year olds) to provide a fun way to get the younger generation into creating their own cool graphics, websites, and even games! The theme of the makerthon is based on being random and kind to anyone and everyone! We really see the value in not only enabling youth to create cool things, but encouraging them to express themselves in kind and random ways.

During the event, highly skilled and professional mentors will help guide them to creating a complete project that they never knew they could make.

The makerthon will be at the NYU Kimmel Center for University Life on November 21st with 7 hours (9:00AM to 4:00PM) of workshops to teach them what they want to learn and build.
